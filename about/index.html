About

Hi! My name is Tom Hale. Right now I’m working as a software engineer at Cognex Corporation outside of Boston, helping build the next generation of industrial barcode readers (yes, that is an industry, and a remarkably profitable one).

I’ve wanted to be an engineer ever since I was five, when I realized that “professional cowboy” wasn’t in the cards. In all those years, I never really asked why. There was no real reason to; why wouldn’t you want to be an engineer? Good job prospects, high pay, stable career – what’s not to like?

But I’ve slowly become more and more convinced that my day job hasn’t really been helping anyone. I have no shame in what I do, and I’m proud of my company (most of the time, at least), but I’m really not sure if that is my “highest” good. No doubt we all need good barcode readers, but I don’t believe that’s our deepest need.1

So, after this crisis, I’ve committed to join Mission Year, a yearlong service program in inner-city Philadelphia. Coronavirus willing, I’ll be starting this August.

Feel free to contact me at my email, justthale@gmail.com. I’d love to hear from you, since the internet is usually a pretty lonely place.

  1. The arrival of coronavirus and the newfound importance of online deliveries has complicated things, especially since Cognex readers are key to most big logistics setups. That being said, my belief stands. If anything, it seems like the pandemic has made our needs even less physical. ↩︎
